Before you come

A few things
worth knowing.

01

Layers are essential

Hood Canal nights get cold even in summer. Bring a warm sleeping bag rated to 40°F or lower, and a waterproof outer layer just in case.

Gear
02

Leave no trace

Pack out everything you bring. The site stays pristine because every guest treats it like their own backyard — please keep that tradition.

Etiquette
03

Best time to visit

Late June through September offers the driest weather. Spring camping is beautiful but come prepared for morning rain and mud.

Timing
04

Wildlife awareness

Store food in your car or a hard-sided container. This is the Pacific Northwest — bears and raccoons are curious neighbors.

Safety
05

Water & power

The site has no hookups. Bring at least 2 liters of water per person per day. A solar charger handles phone batteries well.

Essentials
06

Getting there

GPS works well to the site. Exact directions and any gate codes will be shared after your booking is confirmed via email.

Questions & Answers

Everything you
want to know.

Fill out the booking form above or email us directly. We'll get back to you within 24 hours to confirm availability and share pricing details. YuRu is a private site, so all stays are arranged personally — no online calendars or instant booking.

Pricing is shared after we confirm availability with you. Every dollar you pay goes directly to the Duc Foundation as a scholarship donation — you'll receive a donation receipt. We keep it simple and transparent.

YuRu is open to families, couples, and small groups of close friends. It's not open to the general public — we keep it personal and private. If you know us or were referred by someone who does, you're very welcome here.

The site includes a stone fire ring with firewood, flat cleared ground for up to 3 tents, and full forest privacy. There are no electrical hookups or running water — bring at least 2 liters per person per day. Exact details are shared after booking.

YuRu is in the Hood Canal of Washington State. For privacy reasons we don't share the exact address publicly — full directions and any gate information are sent to you after your booking is confirmed.

Yes — 100% of what you pay goes directly to the Duc Foundation, which provides scholarships for Vietnamese students. We run YuRu with no operational overhead taken from guest payments. You'll receive a donation receipt for your records.

Late June through September offers the driest and warmest conditions — ideal for camping. Spring is beautiful but expect morning rain. Winter camping is possible for experienced campers who don't mind cold, wet Pacific Northwest weather. Whatever the season, always bring layers.
